Still carrying necessities in single-use plastic bags?

Do you not realize that it takes a plastic bag 5000 years to decompose?

Wouldn't you use a time machine to travel back 100 years and live in a society without pollution and plastic?

We may not have a time machine, which is why we came up with the concept of a "cloth bag vending machine."

The project hashtag code green has only a single vision that is to make people use cloth bags instead of single use plastic bags. The Tamilnadu government initiated ‘meendum manjapai’ , which further helps us to make our product reach people in a much larger proportion. In India, an average individual uses 3.65 kilograms of plastic each year. Every day, these discarded plastic bags cause harm to and even kill animals and marine life. While it's not pleasant to see a dead turtle with a plastic bag hanging from its mouth, marine life frequently confuses plastic bags for food.

Since plastic bags are very harmful to the environment, and the supply of cloth bags to the public is minimal.When installed in locations such as grocery stores, shopping centers, super markets, etc., cloth bag vending machines help us to make the supply meet the demand by giving people reusable cloth bags.
